Whole-home filtration, the flagship system.

One system treats every tap in your house, softening the water and filtering out chlorine and chemicals, plus RO at the kitchen sink for drinking.

This is the system most {{SERVICE_AREA_LABEL}} homes need, and it's the same one the brand-name guys sell for two to four times the price. It installs off your main water line, so the better water reaches every faucet, shower, and appliance in the house.

What's actually in it

A single tank holds four media layers, with the control valve on top and the brine (salt) tank beside it:

  • Jacobi coconut-shell carbon removes chlorine and chemicals first, protecting the resin and fixing taste and smell.
  • ResinTech CG10 resin does the ion exchange that softens your water, better for hair, skin, appliances, and your water heater.
  • NSF-certified gravel underbed anchors the system.
  • EnPress fiberglass tank, the most premium vessel in the industry.
The ceramic disc valve is the difference. Every competitor uses plastic that wears out in two to five years. Ours is ceramic, harder than the minerals in your water, tested to a simulated 400-year life and backed by a lifetime warranty.

How it gets installed

A licensed Michigan installer connects the system at your main line, usually within the week of your order. Most installs are clean basement or garage jobs. If your home has a tougher setup, we tell you the adjusted price before any work starts, never after.

Maintenance is simple

Keep two to three bags of salt in the brine tank and check it every few months. Salt is about $7 a bag. The softener has no filters to change; it self-cleans during regeneration. The RO filters get swapped about once a year.
